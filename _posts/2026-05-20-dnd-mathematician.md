The premise of the class: instead of spell slots, your features cost *rolls*. You have a "computation pool" of dice — say, three d6s at level 1, scaling up — and to activate any class ability you spend a die. The die's result determines the magnitude of the effect: roll high, the ability hits hard; roll low, you've burned the die for very little.

What I find interesting is what this does to player decision-making. A spellcaster with slots optimizes for *"did this work?"* — you know what fireball does, you cast fireball, fireball happens. A Mathematician optimizes for *"what's the expected distribution of outcomes if I use this same ability five times in a fight?"* — a different mental loop, more like portfolio management than spellcasting.

What I'm worried about, mechanically:

1. **Spotlight time.** Other players don't want to wait while the Mathematician calculates expected value for every move. Need a way to make decisions fast.
2. **Bad-luck spirals.** A run of low rolls early in a fight could leave the class feeling useless, in a way that wizards don't suffer.
3. **Min-maxing edge.** A player who actually does the math will be much stronger than one who plays on vibes. Is that a feature or a bug?

First playtest is this weekend. I'll write up what broke.
